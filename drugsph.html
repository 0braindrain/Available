Understanding Substance Use in the Philippines

Drug Use Patterns and Demographics

Current Landscape

Key Findings

  • Significant decline from 4 million users in 2016 to 1.67 million in 2019
  • Enhanced treatment programs show 43.49% increase in voluntary admissions
  • Male-to-female ratio remains high at 12:1
  • Most users (30.49%) are 40 years and above

Risk Factors

  • Societal pressures and gender stereotypes
  • Economic stress and unemployment
  • Early exposure (15-19 age group most vulnerable)
  • Limited access to mental health services

Impact on Self, Relationships, and Productivity

Self-Image Impact

  • • Loss of self-esteem and confidence
  • • Physical health deterioration
  • • Mental health complications
  • • Identity and purpose crisis

Relationship Impact

  • • Family breakdown and conflict
  • • Loss of trust and communication
  • • Social isolation and stigma
  • • Domestic violence increase

Productivity Impact

  • • Decreased work performance
  • • Increased absenteeism
  • • Economic burden on families
  • • Reduced economic contribution

Employment Status Among Users

58% of users are employed, highlighting the need for workplace prevention programs
